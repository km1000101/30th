What is Indian Sign Language?

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is the primary sign language used by the deaf community in India. It's a complete, natural language with its own grammar and syntax, distinct from spoken languages.

Understanding ISL

Indian Sign Language is a visual-spatial language that uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to convey meaning. Unlike spoken languages, ISL relies on:

  • Hand Shapes: Different hand configurations represent letters, numbers, and concepts
  • Movement: Direction and motion of hands add meaning to signs
  • Location: Where signs are made relative to the body
  • Facial Expressions: Emotions and grammatical markers
  • Body Language: Posture and positioning enhance communication
